Why WebP Images?

What is a WebP file?

Google introduced the WebP format to help speed up loading times on the internet. WebP enables websites to showcase high-quality images while maintaining smaller file sizes compared to traditional formats like PNG and JPEG.

Users have the option to save WebP files with either lossless or lossy compression. This means images can be compressed without losing data or by losing only some non-essential details. According to Google's developers, lossless WebP images can be up to 26% smaller than PNGs.

You can easily identify a WebP image by its .WEBP extension.

Both file formats compress images to facilitate sharing and storage. However, WebP files are typically much smaller than traditional JPEGs. According to Google's developers, a WebP image with lossy compression can be 25% to 34% smaller than a similar JPEG file.

Does WebP work on all browsers?

After years of progress and refinement, most web browsers now support the WebP format. However, some browsers, such as Internet Explorer, might still have difficulty handling WebP files. It's always a good idea to check Google's most recent compatibility list.

Is WebP an open-source file format?

WebP is an open-source format, allowing anyone to contribute to its development and improvements. Since its launch in 2010, Google's developers have actively encouraged users to provide feedback and suggestions.

Does a WebP image have a maximum size?

WebP images have a maximum size of 16,383 x 16,383 pixels. It's important to keep this in mind when adding WebP images to a website or other platforms.

Are WebP images raster or vector files?

WebP images fall under the category of raster files. Raster files are one of the two primary types of image files, the other being vector files. The key distinction between them is that raster files are made up of individual pixels, while vector graphics are composed of mathematical equations and paths.
